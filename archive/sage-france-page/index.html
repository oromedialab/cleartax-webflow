The France e-invoicing mandate

What you actually need to know

A fast read of the framework, the players, and the dates that matter — before you sign with anyone.

The 5-corner model in one paragraph

All B2B transactions between VAT-registered businesses in France must flow through an accredited Plateforme Agréée (PA) — a private operator certified by DGFiP to issue, receive, validate, and transmit e-invoices on your behalf.

The PPF (Portail Public de Facturation) is the central directory and tax data hub — it routes invoices to the right PA and concentrates e-reporting data for the tax authority. It is not an exchange route. Chorus Pro remains the channel for B2G (public sector) invoicing.

Invoices must be in UBL 2.1, CII, or Factur-X format. Sending a PDF by email — even a well-formatted one — will not be compliant.

ClearTax vs Sage in France: straight answers

The questions we get asked most by finance and tax leaders evaluating providers ahead of September 2026.

Is ClearTax an accredited e-invoicing provider in France?
Yes. ClearTax holds confirmed Plateforme Agréée (PA) status under the DGFiP's official accreditation programme, and is legally authorised to issue, receive, validate, and transmit e-invoices and e-reporting data on behalf of French VAT-registered businesses. The platform is built on the same compliance-grade infrastructure deployed for Peppol-based e-invoicing in Belgium and Croatia, KSeF e-invoicing in Poland, and multiple other EU jurisdictions.
What is the difference between ClearTax and Sage for France e-invoicing?
ClearTax is a global compliance infrastructure provider. Sage is a business software company with a France-native PA built for its own product ecosystem. ClearTax has the edge in multi-country deployment, confirmed PA accreditation, non-Sage ERP integration, and e-reporting automation breadth. Sage has the edge for existing Sage software customers who want the simplest possible compliance path within their current setup.
Is Sage a registered Plateforme Agréée?
Yes — Sage France is a registered Plateforme Agréée. The right question for your evaluation isn't whether each provider is a PA, but which one fits your ERP environment, geographic footprint, and compliance scope.
How quickly can ClearTax integrate with my ERP?
For standard SAP, Oracle, and Microsoft Dynamics configurations, ClearTax typically completes enterprise onboarding in 4–6 weeks. Complex multi-entity or heavily customised ERP environments take longer — a technical scoping call will give you an accurate estimate. ClearTax's AI-powered integration agent delivers 4× faster go-live compared to conventional approaches. Once your ERP is integrated, a single global API unlocks all supported geographies — a new country mandate requires only a configuration update, not a new integration.
What is PPF and why does it matter for choosing a provider?
PPF stands for Portail Public de Facturation — the French government's central invoicing infrastructure managed by DGFiP. Under the 5-corner model, PPF is not an invoice exchange point. It is the national directory that routes invoices to the correct receiving platform, and the data concentrator that collects e-invoicing and e-reporting data from PAs and forwards it to the tax authority. Your PA must have confirmed interoperability with the PPF — a provider that has registered intent but not completed PPF interoperability testing cannot guarantee compliance at go-live.
Does ClearTax support both B2B and B2G e-invoicing in France?
Yes. ClearTax handles domestic B2B e-invoicing through its PA infrastructure and supports B2G invoicing through Chorus Pro connectivity. Businesses dealing with both public sector clients and private sector counterparties — which is most mid-sized and large enterprises — are covered by a single platform.
What experience does ClearTax have with e-invoicing in Europe?
ClearTax operates live e-invoicing mandates in Belgium, Poland, and Croatia, and is expanding across Europe. Globally, the platform has processed over 1B+ e-invoices across India, Saudi Arabia, UAE, and multiple EU jurisdictions. ClearTax's France compliance team tracks DGFiP regulatory updates and translates them into product changes — not periodic patches, but continuous updates aligned to how the mandate evolves.
What are the penalties for non-compliance?
Under France's Finance Law 2026, non-compliance carries:
  • €50 per non-compliant e-invoice
  • €500 per non-compliant e-reporting transmission
Annual caps apply. Beyond the fines, there is an operational risk — trading partners who are compliant will not be able to receive your non-compliant invoices after the mandate goes live. That means delayed payments and disrupted cash flow, which is often more damaging than the penalty itself.
Is ClearTax or Sage better for a France SME?
It depends on your ERP. If you're an SME already running Sage 100 or Sage 50, with invoice volumes under the included thresholds and no cross-border complexity, Sage's bundled PA is the simpler path. If you run any other ERP, have B2C flows, cross-border transactions, or plan to expand beyond France, ClearTax gives you more flexibility, confirmed accreditation, and multi-country coverage without needing a second vendor as you scale.
Which is faster to implement — ClearTax or Sage?
For existing Sage software users: Sage — the PA is already embedded in the product. For everyone else: ClearTax — pre-built connectors for SAP, Oracle, and 60+ other systems bring integration timelines down to 4–6 weeks. For any business not running Sage software, ClearTax is the faster path to a September 2026 go-live.
What is the best Sage alternative in France?
ClearTax is the strongest alternative for businesses running non-Sage ERPs, multinationals that need compliance across multiple countries on a single platform, and companies that need confirmed PA accreditation without provisional caveats. The right choice depends on your ERP environment, geographic footprint, and compliance timeline — but for any business with complexity beyond a single-country Sage deployment, ClearTax is the more scalable option.
