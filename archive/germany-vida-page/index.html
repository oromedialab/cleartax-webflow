Germany E-Invoicing Mandate at Glance

Germany's domestic B2B e-invoicing regime was enacted through the Wachstumschancengesetz (Growth Opportunities Act, in force 27 March 2024) and the BMF-Schreiben of 15 October 2024 (last amended 15 October 2025). It rewrites § 14 UStG: since 1 January 2025, an "E-Rechnung" is a structured electronic invoice conforming to EN 16931. A PDF or paper invoice is now a "sonstige Rechnung", a lesser category.

The mandate rolls out in three phases. Phase 1 (receive) is already binding on every domestic business; Phase 2 and Phase 3 (issue) close out by 1 January 2028.

Live since 1 Jan 2025

Phase 1, Receive obligation

Every German VAT-registered business, including Kleinunternehmer under § 19 UStG and B2C-only sellers, must be able to receive an EN 16931 e-invoice (XRechnung, ZUGFeRD BASIC profile or higher, or Peppol BIS Billing 3.0).

  • Legal basis: § 14 UStG (new), § 27 Abs. 38 UStG (transition)
  • Format: structured EN 16931 (or a format that extracts losslessly to it)
  • Penalty exposure: § 26a Abs. 2 UStG up to €5,000; § 379 AO up to €25,000
From 1 Jan 2027

Phase 2, Issuance, turnover > €800k

Businesses with prior-year (2026) total turnover above €800,000 must issue every domestic B2B invoice as a structured EN 16931 e-invoice. The transitional permission to send a "sonstige Rechnung" (PDF, paper) for B2B expires for this cohort on 31 December 2026.

  • EDI procedures not meeting EN 16931 may continue until 31 Dec 2027
  • Buyer's input-VAT recovery hinges on a compliant E-Rechnung, § 15 UStG
From 1 Jan 2028

Phase 3, Issuance, all businesses

All remaining German VAT-registered businesses must issue structured B2B e-invoices, Mittelstand and SMEs included. Kleinunternehmer under § 19 UStG remain exempt from issuance (§ 34a UStDV) but must still receive E-Rechnungen.

  • Final transitional window for sub-€800k issuers expires 31 Dec 2027
  • All EDI procedures must conform to EN 16931 by this date

The German e-invoice format families

XRechnung

A pure-XML CIUS of EN 16931 maintained by KoSIT. The mandatory format for federal B2G via the ZRE / OZG-RE portals since 27 November 2020. Fully valid for B2B from 1 January 2025.

ZUGFeRD (BASIC upwards)

A hybrid PDF/A-3 carrying the EN 16931 XML, maintained by FeRD. Profiles BASIC, EN 16931, EXTENDED, XRECHNUNG are EN 16931-conformant. MINIMUM and BASIC WL are not valid as a German E-Rechnung.

Peppol BIS Billing 3.0

The OpenPeppol four-corner profile, accepted as an EN 16931-conformant channel for German B2B and B2G. Common for cross-border European trade and the default for many large enterprises.

GoBD: the 10-year archival rule that catches finance teams off-guard

Under the GoBD (last amended 14 July 2025 to reflect the obligatory E-Rechnung), every issued and received e-invoice must be archived in its original structured format for 10 years, with full audit trail, unalterability, and machine-readable access for a Betriebsprüfung. PDF-only or screen-print archives are insufficient.

What is ViDA?

EU-wide legislation adopted March 2025. Every member state. Every cross-border B2B invoice. From July 2030.

01

Digital Reporting Requirements (DRR)

  • Mandatory structured e-invoicing for all intra-EU B2B transactions
  • E-invoice issued within 10 days of the chargeable event; transaction data reported to the seller's national tax authority, which feeds the central EU VIES
  • Applies to all 27 member states from 1 July 2030, no opt-out
Mandatory from July 2030
02

Single VAT Registration (SVR)

  • One Stop Shop (OSS) extended to cover all B2B cross-border supplies across the EU
  • Businesses no longer need separate VAT registration in every country they sell into
  • One registration. One return. One point of contact with tax authorities.
Reduces compliance overhead
03

Platform Economy VAT Liability

  • Digital platforms, short-term rental, ride-hailing, gig economy, become the deemed VAT supplier
  • Platform collects and remits VAT directly; individual sellers no longer liable
  • Closes the VAT gap created by unregistered sellers on marketplace platforms

Is ClearTax a member of VeR? +
Yes. ClearTax is a member of VeR, Verband elektronische Rechnung e.V. (the German chapter of the GENA/EESPA e-invoicing alliance), which develops and promotes electronic invoicing standards in Germany. Our membership reflects that our XRechnung and ZUGFeRD implementations follow published German e-invoicing standards. We can share the VeR reference on the briefing call.
Is ViDA actually law yet? Or is this an early-mover argument? +
ViDA was formally adopted by the EU Council on 11 March 2025 as Council Directive (EU) 2025/516. Phase 1 implementation deadline is 1 July 2030 for cross-border DRR. This is not speculative, it is binding EU law with a published timetable. Germany is now in national transposition. Validate the directive text on EUR-Lex; the dates we're showing match.
We already have an XRechnung project scoped for 2027. Should we tear it up? +
Not necessarily. Two honest paths: (a) ship the 2027 project as planned and run a parallel ViDA evaluation in 2028, accepting you'll likely re-platform; or (b) pause the RFP for six weeks and re-scope it as a ViDA-ready selection. We don't recommend (a) when total programme spend over five years is the metric, but we don't have your numbers. Bring them to the briefing and we'll model both honestly.
