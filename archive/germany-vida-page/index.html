What is ViDA?

EU-wide legislation adopted March 2025. Every member state. Every cross-border B2B invoice. From July 2030.

01

Digital Reporting Requirements (DRR)

  • Mandatory structured e-invoicing for all intra-EU B2B transactions
  • Every intra-EU B2B invoice reported to Central EU VIES database within 10 days
  • Applies to all 27 member states from 1 July 2030 — no opt-out
Mandatory from July 2030
02

Single VAT Registration (SVR)

  • One Stop Shop (OSS) extended to cover all B2B cross-border supplies across the EU
  • Businesses no longer need separate VAT registration in every country they sell into
  • One registration. One return. One point of contact with tax authorities.
Reduces compliance overhead
03

Platform Economy VAT Liability

  • Digital platforms — short-term rental, ride-hailing, gig economy — become the deemed VAT supplier
  • Platform collects and remits VAT directly; individual sellers no longer liable
  • Closes the VAT gap created by unregistered sellers on marketplace platforms

The Strategic Question

Why ViDA 2030 changes the platform decision

The 2027 mandate is a format change — XRechnung instead of PDF, structured via a certified system. Any reasonable e-invoicing tool handles the format. If 2027 were the only deadline, you could buy a point solution and move on.

ViDA 2030 is a data-flow change. Every cross-border B2B invoice triggers a real-time report within 10 days, in EN 16931 form, with line-level detail. A 2027-only XRechnung tool can't do this. It wasn't designed for the wire.

CFO & Head of Tax FAQ

Six questions we hear in every first call

Direct answers. No marketing language. If a question isn't on this list, ask it on the briefing.

Is ClearTax listed on VerENA? +
Yes. ClearTax is listed on VerENA, the Verband eBusiness-Standards Germany — the industry association that defines and promotes e-invoicing standards in Germany. The listing confirms our XRechnung and ZUGFeRD implementations meet published German e-invoicing standards. We can share the VerENA reference on the briefing call.
Is ViDA actually law yet? Or is this an early-mover argument? +
ViDA was formally adopted by the EU Council on 11 March 2025 as Council Directive (EU) 2025/516. Phase 1 implementation deadline is 1 July 2030 for cross-border DRR. This is not speculative — it is binding EU law with a published timetable. Germany is now in national transposition. Validate the directive text on EUR-Lex; the dates we're showing match.
We already have an XRechnung project scoped for 2027. Should we tear it up? +
Not necessarily. Two honest paths: (a) ship the 2027 project as planned and run a parallel ViDA evaluation in 2028, accepting you'll likely re-platform; or (b) pause the RFP for six weeks and re-scope it as a ViDA-ready selection. Path (b) typically adds 8–10% to the 2027 budget and removes the 2029 re-platform cost line. We don't recommend (a) when total programme spend over five years is the metric — but we don't have your numbers. Bring them to the briefing and we'll model both honestly.
Does the platform post into our SAP / Datev / Oracle without changes? +
Yes — and this is the question we hear most. The ClearTax SAP add-on is certified on the SAP Store for both S/4HANA and ECC. We have native connectors for Oracle, Workday, Sage, and Datev. No changes to your COA, no changes to your close calendar, no custom ABAP. Typical integration in production is 4–8 weeks. Your auditor sees the same general ledger entries they see today.
What does a German Betriebsprüfung (tax audit) look like with this in place? +
The archive is 10-year GoBD-compliant, EN 16931-conformant, with every lifecycle status logged (Issued → Sent → Received → Validated → Approved → Paid). The exception command centre exposes a "tax audit view" that walks an inspector through the trail in the order they'll ask for it. We can put you in touch with a customer that has completed a Betriebsprüfung on this view as a reference.
What are the actual penalties for non-compliance with the German e-invoicing mandate? +
The headline penalty under German law is up to €5,000 per infringement for failing to issue or receive structured e-invoices as required, with provisions under §14 UStG. The cap is not what should worry CFOs. The real exposure is input-VAT recoverability: a non-compliant supplier invoice may not be deductible under §15 UStG. For a buyer with €500M+ in B2B spend, even a small percentage of non-compliant inbound invoices creates a seven-figure cash-flow hit, every year, until fixed.
