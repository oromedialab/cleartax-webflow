The BFSI reality

Margin-based banking income is exempt under DL8.46.1, but only if you can prove the supply is margin-based at the product master, not at invoice time. A retail bank with AED 8B annual interest income plus AED 600M fee income needs to distinguish exempt-margin from standard-rated fees on every line, every cycle, against millions of customers.

The ClearTax solution

ClearTax's product master separates margin-based from fee-based income at SKU level so the line item carries the right tax category every time. Interest products get coded as E plus DL8.46.1, fee products as S, and a quarterly board-level exempt-vs-taxable income reconciliation ties straight back to the VAT return.

So what?

Misclassify interest income as standard-rated and you've manufactured a 5% phantom VAT liability on AED 8B of exempt income, billions in over-collection. Misclassify a fee as exempt and you've under-collected the actual VAT due. Both errors compound silently across millions of customer accounts.

The BFSI reality

Most banks have 200+ fee SKUs across cards, accounts, lending, and FX. A UAE bank with 1.8M retail accounts charging AED 25 monthly SMS alerts plus AED 75 quarterly account fees generates 5.4M fee invoices per quarter. Each needs the correct billing period (BT-73/74), the right frequency code, and a clean envelope through the ASP. Monthly fee cycles, standing instructions, and subscription-style banking products are the second-most-common BFSI rejection after wrong tax category.

The ClearTax solution

ClearTax's bulk billing engine generates millions of fee invoices in compliant PINT-AE format without breaking core banking. BT-73/74 are auto-filled from source metadata, the right envelope is attached every cycle, and the streaming API hands off to your ASP with monthly summary aggregation per customer to avoid micro-invoice overhead.

The BFSI reality
