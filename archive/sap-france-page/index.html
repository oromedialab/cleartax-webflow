What's the case for a native SAP add-on?

If your goal is to move from middleware-and-spreadsheet workarounds to a fully embedded e-invoicing posture inside SAP, ClearTax is built for that. We don't sit outside SAP poking at it through batch jobs — we deploy a certified ABAP add-on inside your SAP system, shipped through STMS like any other transport.

This means data extraction happens via standard SAP objects (VBRK, BSEG, BKPF, ACDOCA), validation runs inside your SAP environment, and your tax team works from a Fiori-style cockpit in the same client they already use.

The result: every invoice your SAP system creates becomes a compliant, structured e-invoice — in the format your jurisdiction demands — without leaving SAP.

A compliant e-invoice, end to end in SAP

Six capabilities, one transport. Here's how data moves from billing document to tax authority — and what ClearTax does at every step.

  • Submission to PA / PPF / KSeF
  • Status sync back to SAP

SAP-native ingestion

Pre-built RICEF objects (Reports, Interfaces, Conversions, Enhancements, Forms) deployed via STMS. ClearTax reads from VBRK, BSEG, BKPF, ACDOCA and KNA1/LFA1 — no flat-file exports, no third-party middleware. BAdI hooks for output determination ensure invoices route through compliance before printing.

IDoc INVOIC02 OData v2/v4 BAPI_INCOMINGINVOICE BTE 00503301

Format engine

Every mandate has its own format. ClearTax's format engine converts your SAP output into UBL 2.1, CII, Factur-X (France); FA(2) XML (KSeF Poland); ZATCA Phase-2 XML (Saudi); FTA UBL (UAE); Peppol BIS 3.0 (Belgium and cross-border). One source object, every output format.

UBL 2.1 CII Factur-X Peppol BIS FA(2)

Real-time status sync

Every invoice carries its lifecycle state back into SAP. The 14-status DGFiP lifecycle, KSeF UPO references, ZATCA QR codes, FTA acknowledgement IDs — all written back to dedicated Z-tables linked to your billing documents. Your AR team sees compliance status without ever leaving SAP.

Fiori-style cockpit

A custom Fiori application gives tax and AR teams a single pane of glass: failed validations, pending submissions, status by company code, by VAT registration, by mandate. Mass resubmission, exception management, and audit trails — all inside SAP launchpad, with role-based authorisations via PFCG.

FAQ

SAP customers, straight answers

The questions we get asked most by SAP basis teams, FI leads and CIOs scoping their e-invoicing project.

How does ClearTax actually deploy into our SAP system?
ClearTax ships as a native ABAP add-on in the /CLEARTAX/* namespace. Deployment is straightforward for any SAP basis team:
  1. SAINT/SPAM import of the add-on package into your development client
  2. Configuration via SPRO transactions — company codes in scope, mandate selection, output determination, RFC connection to ClearTax Compliance Cloud
  3. Field mapping in the Fiori cockpit — point-and-click for standard fields, BAdI hooks for custom Z-fields
  4. Transport through STMS to QA → Prod, exactly like any other change
No core SAP modifications. No SAP Note prerequisites beyond standard support pack levels. SAP support for your base system is unaffected.
Which SAP versions are supported?
  • SAP ECC 6.0 — supported from EHP7 onwards (earlier EHPs supported on request, may require a backport effort)
  • SAP S/4HANA on-premise — 1709, 1809, 1909, 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023
  • SAP S/4HANA Cloud, Private Edition — fully supported via standard transport landscape
  • SAP S/4HANA Cloud, Public Edition — supported via SAP BTP-side connector + ClearTax API (no in-system code)
  • SAP Business One & ByDesign — supported via lighter API-based connectors
For multi-version landscapes (e.g. ECC central with S/4HANA satellites), the same add-on can be deployed across all systems with a single configuration model.
How does the integration handle our heavily customised SAP environment?
This is where most SAP integrations break, and it's why ClearTax was built differently. The add-on uses BAdI hooks at every key extension point — output determination, document creation, status update — so you can extend the standard logic without touching ClearTax code. Custom Z-fields, Z-tables, and bespoke output types are surfaced in the field-mapping cockpit and bound to e-invoice elements through configuration. For truly unusual cases, the AI-powered integration agent reads your output determination, infers the likely mapping, and produces a draft for your SAP team to review — typically cutting field-mapping effort by 70%.
How does this compare to SAP Document Compliance (DRC)?
SAP Document and Reporting Compliance (DRC) is SAP's first-party tool for some mandates. It works well in environments where every supported country is covered and you're willing to license, configure and maintain each country pack separately. ClearTax is the alternative when:
  • You need mandates that DRC doesn't yet cover (or covers with limitations)
  • You want a single platform for all geographies — not separate country licences
  • You need confirmed PA accreditation in France (DRC does not act as a PA)
  • You want a faster go-live than DRC + an integration partner typically delivers
  • You want one vendor handling both submission and the full e-reporting cycle
Many of our customers run DRC for one or two countries and ClearTax for everything else — they coexist fine.
What is the architecture between SAP and ClearTax Compliance Cloud?
Outbound (SAP → ClearTax): the ABAP add-on triggers on billing-document creation (typically via BTE 00503301 or output determination NACE) and pushes a structured payload over an authenticated HTTPS RFC destination to the ClearTax Compliance Cloud. Real-time for high-priority flows; batch via IDoc INVOIC02 for bulk runs.

ClearTax cloud: converts to the target format (UBL 2.1 / CII / Factur-X / FA(2) / Peppol BIS), validates against schema and business rules, submits to the tax authority (PA / PPF / KSeF / ZATCA / FTA), and persists the lifecycle status.

Inbound (ClearTax → SAP): status writeback through the same RFC, updating Z-tables linked to VBRK-VBELN. The Fiori cockpit surfaces this to your AR team without leaving SAP launchpad.

Hosting: ClearTax runs in the geographic region your data residency requirements demand (EU, GCC, India, etc.).
How is data residency and security handled?
ClearTax is ISO 27001 and SOC 2 Type II certified. Data residency is regional — EU customer data stays in EU AWS regions; UAE data in AWS Middle East; KSA data in-Kingdom — and never crosses the boundary. Transit is TLS 1.3, at rest is AES-256. SAP-side authentication is via RFC destinations with rotating tokens, optionally hardened with mTLS. Role-based access in the Fiori cockpit is mapped to SAP PFCG roles, so your authorisation team controls who sees what.
What does a typical 4–6 week deployment actually look like?
  1. Week 1 — Scope & design. Workshop with your SAP FI/SD/MM leads. Output determination review. Custom field inventory. Sign-off on field-mapping draft from the AI agent.
  2. Week 2 — Transport to DEV. Add-on imported. RFC destinations configured. Field mapping refined. First test invoices submitted to sandbox tax authority.
  3. Week 3 — Transport to QA. UAT with your tax and AR teams using real production-shape data. Edge cases identified and resolved.
  4. Week 4 — Parallel run. Production invoices submitted in parallel (mirror mode) — comparing ClearTax output to your existing process.
  5. Week 5–6 — Go-live. Production cutover. Hypercare period with daily check-ins. Handover to ongoing support.
Multi-entity, multi-country, or heavily customised environments take longer — a 10-week plan is realistic for 5+ company codes across 3+ mandates. Honest scoping happens on the first call.
What happens when the mandate changes — does that need a new SAP project?
No. Mandate changes are handled in the ClearTax cloud, not in your SAP system. When DGFiP publishes External Specs v3.2, when ZATCA changes invoice schemas, when KSeF adds a new validation rule — ClearTax updates the format engine and validation library, and your add-on continues sending the same structured payload it always did. The translation layer absorbs the change. Your SAP team rarely needs to ship a new transport. This is the core architectural value of putting the mandate-specific complexity outside SAP.
Can we test against a sandbox tax authority before going live?
Yes — and we strongly recommend it. ClearTax's DEV and QA environments connect to the official sandbox endpoints for every supported mandate (DGFiP sandbox, KSeF demo, ZATCA fatoora simulation, FTA pre-production). You can run your real SAP invoices through the full pipeline in QA before flipping production. This is also where AI-mapped fields get validated against real schema responses.
