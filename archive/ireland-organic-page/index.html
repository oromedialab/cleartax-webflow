Key requirements

Ireland e-invoicing mandate at a glance

Everything your business needs to know about Ireland's e-invoicing and VAT modernisation rules.

Model

Transmission model

Decentralised five-corner Peppol model - no central portal. From Nov 2028, a subset of B2B invoice data is also reported to Revenue in near real time.

Scope

Applicability

Phase 1 (2028) covers VAT-registered large corporates. All businesses must be able to receive e-invoices; intra-EU traders follow in 2029.

Format

Acceptable formats

Structured EN 16931, typically Peppol BIS Billing 3.0 (XML) over Peppol. PDFs and scans do not count as e-invoices.

Transmit

Transmission methods

Via the Peppol network through an accredited Access Point such as ClearTax, registered on the SMP - not by email.

Signatures & storage

Signatures & archival

Digital signatures are not mandatory - ensure authenticity via controls, EDI or e-signature. Retain VAT records for 6 years.

Penalties

Penalties

No dedicated B2B penalty regime yet, but existing VAT rules apply: a €4,000 fixed penalty (S.115 VATCA 2010) for invoicing or record-keeping failures, plus interest and tax-geared penalties.
