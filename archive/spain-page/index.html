Spain 2026 Guide

What you need to know

The Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) is rolling out mandatory e-invoicing through its Electronic Invoicing/Receipting System (EIS), with the first compliance deadline set for 31 December 2026.

Phase 1 covers large taxpayers, e-commerce businesses, and Computerized Accounting System (CAS) users, who must generate invoices in structured JSON or XML format and transmit data to the BIR in near-real time, within 3 calendar days of each transaction.

Micro-taxpayers are currently exempt, and Phase 2 timelines for remaining taxpayer groups will be confirmed through future BIR regulations.

About Spain e-Invoicing

About Spain e-Invoicing

Authority

Administered by the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) via the Electronic Invoicing/Receipting System (EIS).

Scope

Phase 1 covers Large Taxpayers, e-commerce businesses, CAS users, and exporters. Micro-taxpayers are exempt from mandatory requirements.

Format

Invoices must be issued in structured XML or JSON format aligned to the BIR's data schema.

Transmission

Invoice data must be submitted to the BIR's EIS in real time or near-real time, no later than 3 calendar days after the transaction.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is e-invoicing in Spain? +
Under the Crea y Crece Law (Ley 18/2022), B2B e-invoicing is the electronic exchange of structured, machine-readable invoices between businesses established in Spain, transmitted via private platforms or the AEAT public solution in an EN 16931-aligned format such as Facturae, UBL or CII.
Who must comply, and when? +
Rollout is phased from the Ministerial Order's entry into force (expected ~1 October 2026). Large companies (turnover above €8M) ~12 months later (~October 2027); all other businesses and sole traders ~24 months later (~October 2028). Veri*Factu software rules apply from 1 January 2027 (companies) and 1 July 2027 (self-employed).
What's the difference between Crea y Crece and Veri*Factu? +
They are parallel but independent. Crea y Crece governs the exchange of B2B e-invoices and invoice-status reporting; Veri*Factu (RD 1007/2023) governs the integrity and immutability of billing software. Most businesses will need to address both.
What format must e-invoices be in? +
Structured and machine-readable, aligned to EN 16931. Facturae, UBL, CII and EDIFACT can be used between private platforms; a faithful copy ("copia fiel") is sent to the AEAT public solution. PDFs, Excel and paper no longer qualify once the mandate applies.
How does this relate to SII? +
SII (Suministro Inmediato de Información) has required large taxpayers to report ledgers to AEAT within 4 days since 2017. It continues alongside the new B2B regime, and the two are expected to be consolidated over time.
What are the penalties for non-compliance? +
Producing or using non-certified billing software can carry penalties up to €50,000 per year under Spain's anti-fraud rules. Failing to offer or issue B2B e-invoices under Crea y Crece can trigger fines up to €10,000. Final Crea y Crece penalties will be confirmed in the Ministerial Order.
How does ClearTax help businesses comply? +
ClearTax integrates with your ERP, billing or POS system, converts invoices into the required EN 16931 structured format, routes them through the AEAT public solution or private platforms, handles 4-day invoice-status reporting, and provides automated error management with local support.
