France e-invoicing at a glance

From 2026, all taxable B2B transactions in France will be required to follow mandatory e-invoicing and e-reporting norms. The French DGFiP has adopted a Y-model architecture where businesses exchange invoices via certified PDPs or through the public Chorus Pro platform.

Y-model infrastructure

A government-led "Y-model" system connects businesses through certified PDPs and the public Chorus Pro directory for routing, validation and lifecycle management.

E-reporting & payment reporting

Tax-relevant B2B and B2C transaction data must be reported to the DGFiP. E-reporting covers domestic and cross-border flows for VAT pre-filling and fraud detection.

14 status e-invoice lifecycle

Each e-invoice passes through up to 14 mandatory status stages from issuance to payment. From deposit to final settlement, every state transition is tracked and reported to the French tax authority for audit and reconciliation.

Frequently asked questions.

What is the e-invoicing and e-reporting mandate in France?
France is mandating B2B e-invoicing and e-reporting starting September 2026. All businesses will be required to receive e-invoices, while the obligation to issue will be phased in based on company size. E-reporting covers B2C and cross-border B2B transaction data submission to the DGFiP.
Which businesses must comply with the new rules in France?
All VAT-registered businesses in France must comply. Large and mid-sized enterprises must issue e-invoices from September 2026, while small businesses have until September 2027. All businesses must be able to receive e-invoices from September 2026.
What invoice formats are accepted under the French e-invoicing regime?
France accepts three structured electronic invoice formats: UBL (Universal Business Language), CII (Cross Industry Invoice), and Factur-X (a hybrid format combining a PDF with embedded XML data). All formats must comply with the EN 16931 European standard.
How does ClearTax help with compliance under the mandates?
ClearTax provides an end-to-end solution: PDP and Chorus Pro connectivity, automatic format conversion (UBL, CII, Factur-X), 14-status lifecycle tracking, e-reporting automation, and ERP integration with SAP, Oracle, and other systems.
What must a business consider when selecting an e-invoicing solution or platform (PDP) in France?
Key considerations include PDP certification status, support for all three accepted formats (UBL, CII, Factur-X), ability to handle the 14-status lifecycle, e-reporting capabilities, ERP integration options, multi-country support, and data security certifications.
How does data validation work in the French e-invoicing ecosystem?
Data validation occurs at multiple stages: the issuing PDP validates the invoice structure and data against DGFiP rules, Chorus Pro validates routing information and business identifiers, and the receiving PDP performs final validation before delivery to the buyer.
What security measures must e-invoicing platforms comply with in France?
Platforms must comply with GDPR, maintain ISO 27001 certification, undergo regular VAPT assessments, and ensure SOC 2 Type II compliance. Data must be hosted in EU-compliant data centres with encryption at rest and in transit.

Technical requirements include API connectivity for real-time invoice exchange, support for UBL/CII/Factur-X formats, ability to handle the 14-status lifecycle updates, and digital signature/seal capabilities. ClearTax handles all these requirements out of the box.
