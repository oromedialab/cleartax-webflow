The RE reality

A single invoice from a mixed-use development can include a commercial unit taxed at standard rate (5%), a residential unit that is fully VAT-exempt, bare land that carries a different exemption category, and a maintenance charge that falls entirely outside VAT scope. Under UAE e-invoicing, all four must be classified at the individual line level, not averaged or bundled at the invoice total. Before e-invoicing, developers reconciled these differences internally at quarter-end. Now the FTA receives every invoice in real time. A miscoded category on 500 lease invoices means 500 instant failures before close of business, with no correction window.

So what? AED 100 penalty per failed invoice. The correction path (Credit Note plus re-invoice) is a permanent record in the FTA system. 500 leases miscoded means 1,000 correction events, all visible, all audit-attracting.

The ClearTax solution

VAT category codes are assigned and validated at line-item level, never rolled up to invoice level. Exempt lines are auto-matched to the correct Tax Exemption Reason Code at master-data load, so misclassified items never reach the gateway.

The RE reality

There is no provisional invoice category in PINT-AE. Every off-plan milestone payment demand, including estimates, must be issued as a full valid e-invoice (Type 380). The RE reality

A commercial landlord with 1,200 units issues around 14,400 invoices a year. Each lease runs on a recurring billing cycle (monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, or annual) with post-dated cheques settling each instalment. Under Article 25 of the VAT law, the tax point aligns to the cheque due date, not the lease start date or the invoice issue date. The continuous supply flag must be set on every periodic invoice, and the contract reference must link back to the master lease.

So what? Annual leases with post-dated cheques are an operational minefield. Tax-point misalignment between cheque due date and invoice date is a quarter-end reconciliation nightmare. Two FTEs spend their month managing what should be a scheduled job.

The construction reality

Every Running Account (RA) bill tied to a construction milestone must carry TransactionTypeCode position 5 set to 1 (XXXX1XXX). Without this flag, the FTA reads each RA bill as a standalone completed transaction: no link to the master contract, no payment sequence context. The construction reality

Traditional construction billing treated the invoice as a commercial opening position: retention deducted at the bottom, unapproved variations embedded, figures subject to negotiation. Under e-invoicing, the moment an invoice is transmitted through the ASP, it is a filed tax record. Retention must be a separately classified line item. Unapproved variations cannot appear on a live invoice. Any adjustment requires a formal Electronic Credit Note, fully visible to the FTA. For a contractor invoicing AED 50M for a structural milestone with 10% retention, that means AED 45M payable plus AED 5M retention allowance on a single bill.

So what? Negotiation must happen before transmission, not after. A schema-invalid Credit Note from a subcontractor breaks your input VAT recovery on both sides of the transaction. Your AP team has no recourse once the payment run is processed against an invalid document.
