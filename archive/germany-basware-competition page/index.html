Germany's E-Invoicing Mandate

The Wachstumschancengesetz Timeline — Already in Motion

Germany passed the Growth Opportunities Act in March 2024, introducing a phased B2B e-invoicing mandate for all domestic VAT-registered businesses. The deadlines are locked.

1 January 2025
Reception Mandatory
All businesses must be capable of receiving structured e-invoices. Rejecting an EN 16931-compliant invoice is no longer legally permitted.
1 January 2027
Large Enterprises Issue
Businesses with annual turnover exceeding €800,000 must issue e-invoices. Paper and unstructured PDFs are no longer valid.
1 January 2028
All Businesses Issue
Every business, regardless of size or turnover, must issue structured e-invoices for domestic B2B transactions.
July 2030
ViDA Goes Live
The EU's ViDA initiative introduces real-time digital VAT reporting across all member states. Platforms built for CTC today won't need to rebuild.
1
Accepted formats — XRechnung (pure XML), ZUGFeRD 2.1+ (hybrid PDF+XML), and Peppol BIS 3.0. All must comply with EN 16931.
2
GoBD archiving required — e-invoices must be stored in original structured format for a minimum of 8 years, unalterable and audit-ready.
3
VAT deductions at risk — non-compliant invoices mean denied VAT claims. Buyers will demand compliant invoices to protect their own input tax credits.
