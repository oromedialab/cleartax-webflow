About the event

A new era of Philippine tax & finance operations

At "Philippines E-Invoicing as a Tax Transformation Lever", we explore how the BIR's e-invoicing mandate under RR 11-2025 is set to reshape tax and finance operations for Large Taxpayers, e-commerce operators, and enterprises running Computerized Accounting Systems.

With the 31 December 2026 deadline less than 18 months away and EIS Certification, Permit to Transmit, structured JSON invoicing, and 3-day sales data transmission all becoming mandatory businesses must move beyond compliance and rethink how ERP, tax, and e-invoicing systems work together. Early decisions will define long-term success.

Join industry leaders as they share practical insights on what to expect, what to get right before go-live, and how to evaluate and build a scalable, future-ready tax tech stack for the Philippines and the wider Southeast Asia footprint.

Why attend

4 compelling reasons to be in the room

Built for CFOs, CTOs, Tax Heads, and Finance leaders preparing for the BIR mandate.

01

Understand what's changing

Get a clear view of RR 11-2025, RR 26-2025, and the electronic sales reporting rules that follow including what the 31 December 2026 deadline means for Large Taxpayers, e-commerce operators, and CAS users.

02

Prepare systems, data, and BIR filings

Learn what enterprises must get right early from ERP alignment and master data readiness to CAS re-registration, EIS Certification, and Permit to Transmit to avoid costly compliance gaps in the final months before go-live.

03

Build the right tax-tech architecture

Understand how to evaluate the layers of your compliance stack ERP, middleware, e-invoicing, archival across integration depth, scalability, security, and long-term total cost of ownership.

04

Learn from global e-invoicing mandates

Gain insights from mature and emerging e-invoicing markets India, Malaysia, Saudi Arabia, the UAE and apply relevant lessons to the Philippine context. What worked. What broke. What to plan for.
