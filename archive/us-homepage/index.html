Where you're exposed

The US isn't mandated. Your global entities might already be.

US federal procurement already requires structured digital invoicing. Beyond that, there's no national B2B mandate yet — but "no mandate at home" doesn't mean "no exposure." If you have a subsidiary, branch or trading partner in any market below, e-invoicing compliance is already someone's job.

🇺🇸

United States

Low pressure today
No federal B2B mandate; federal agency procurement requires digital invoicing via IPP.
For a US parent: low direct pressure today, but Peppol-based exchange is expanding fast.
🇪🇺

European Union

In scope now
France, Belgium, Germany, Poland — mandatory or phasing in through 2026–2028, ahead of ViDA's 2030 cross-border rules.
For a US parent: any EU subsidiary or EU-based supplier relationship puts you in scope now.
🇸🇦

Saudi Arabia

Live
Live clearance/reporting mandates, phased rollout continuing through 2026.
For a US parent: regional offices must integrate with ZATCA — or the UAE's Peppol-based model.
🇲🇾

Malaysia & Singapore

Phasing in
Real-time reporting (MyInvois) and Peppol-based InvoiceNow, phasing by revenue threshold.
For a US parent: entities crossing the revenue threshold face imminent go-live dates.
🇮🇳

India

Live
Real-time IRN generation mandatory for B2B.
For a US parent: no IRN, no valid input tax credit — a direct cash-flow hit.
🌎

Latin America

Expanding
Expanding or long-established clearance/reporting systems.
For a US parent: growing markets where non-compliance carries real penalties.
