RR 11-2025 in 30 seconds

The mandate has two tracks. You need both.

Revenue Regulation 11-2025 fundamentally changes how Philippine businesses issue, sign, and report invoices. PDFs no longer qualify. Email no longer qualifies.

TRACK A · ISSUANCE

Structured JSON, JWS-signed, BIR-registered

Every invoice must be a structured JSON file, digitally signed with JWS using RS256. The issuing software must hold a valid Acknowledgement Certificate from BIR.

TRACK B · REPORTING

EIS transmission within 3 calendar days

The same signed payload must reach BIR's Electronic Invoicing System within 3 calendar days. Miss the window and you're non-compliant — even with a perfectly formatted invoice.

FAQ

Quick answers, straight from compliance.

Is my company in scope? +
If you're a Large Taxpayer, run an e-commerce business, or use ERP/accounting software, you're in scope under RR 11-2025 — regardless of revenue. And if any branch is in scope, all locations must comply.
What's the deadline? +
Wave 1 is December 31, 2026. Large Taxpayers, e-commerce businesses, and ERP users must issue compliant e-invoices by this date. Wave 2 (exporters, incentive-based) follows under a separate regulation.
What format does BIR require? +
Structured JSON, signed with JWS using RS256. PDFs are explicitly excluded under RR 11-2025. Plain email invoices are also invalid.
Will it integrate with our ERP? +
Yes. ClearTax integrates with SAP (R3, HANA, B1, ECC), Oracle (EBS, Fusion, NetSuite, JD Edwards), Microsoft Dynamics, Shopify, and 95+ custom ERPs. Our AI-powered connector maps your data automatically — setup takes days, not months.
What happens if we miss the deadline? +
Non-compliant invoices are invalidated. You lose input VAT deductions. BIR publishes lists of non-compliant companies. And your Acknowledgement Certificate can be revoked on audit.
