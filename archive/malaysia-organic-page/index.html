FAQ

Your Questions, Answered

Common questions about e-invoicing mandates, ClearTax solutions and implementation timelines.

What is the current status of e-invoicing in Malaysia? +

E-invoicing is now mandatory for most of the businesses in Malaysia. All companies with annual turnover between RM 1 million and RM 5 million must generate e-invoices for B2B, B2G, and B2C transactions from 1 January 2026 (LHDN announced relaxation until 31 December 2027). Also, the Businesses with annual turnover below RM 1 million are fully exempt from e-invoicing following the Prime Minister's announcement on 7 December 2025.

What are the key features of ClearTax e-invoicing software for Malaysia? +

ClearTax is an MDEC-accredited e-invoicing provider with: ✔ Seamless Integration — compatible with any ERP or business system. ✔ Multi-Channel Support — combining online and physical sales into compliant e-invoices. ✔ Buyer Portal — web and app platform for buyers to generate e-invoices via self-service. ✔ Real-Time Validation — ensuring compliance with the MyInvois IRBM system.

How can businesses comply with Phase 2 and Phase 3 of the e-invoicing mandate? +

To comply: ✔ Confirm your obligation based on annual turnover. ✔ Use a MyInvois-compliant software such as ClearTax. ✔ Ensure the UBL 2.1 (XML or JSON) format is used. ✔ Obtain a digital certificate to sign e-invoices. ✔ Train your staff on the e-invoicing process.

What are the benefits of e-invoicing for businesses in Malaysia? +

✔ Streamlined Operations — Automates invoicing and reduces manual work. ✔ Improved Cash Flow — Faster payments and fewer billing disputes. ✔ Better Tax Compliance — More accurate and efficient tax reporting. ✔ Cost Savings — Reduced paper usage and compliance overhead.

Which businesses need to implement e-invoicing in Malaysia? +

  • Businesses with annual turnover above RM 100 million (since 1 August 2024)
  • Businesses between RM 25 million – RM 100 million (since 1 January 2025)
  • Businesses between RM 5 million – RM 25 million (since 1 July 2025)
  • Businesses between RM 1 million – RM 5 million (from 1 January 2026, with relaxation until 31 December 2027)
  • Businesses with annual turnover below RM 1 million are fully exempt (no e-invoicing obligation)

How do I choose the right e-invoicing solution in Malaysia? +

✔ Compliance — Ensure the solution is MDEC-accredited and meets LHDN standards. ✔ Integration — Seamless support with your ERP and business systems. ✔ Scalability — Able to support current and future transaction volumes. ✔ Local Support — Expertise and a local office in Malaysia.
