Section 1 of 4

Regulatory Awareness & Phase Readiness

Q1Has your organisation been formally notified by the Oman Tax Authority (OTA) of your Fawtara phase and go-live deadline?
Q2How well does your finance and IT leadership understand Fawtara's technical requirements — the Peppol PINT-OM format, the OTA-accredited ASP obligation, and the 10-year structured archiving mandate?
Section 2 of 4

Technical Infrastructure

Q3Can your ERP or billing system generate e-invoices in the OTA-required Peppol PINT-OM (structured XML) format automatically — or does it need significant development or third-party integration?
Q4Have you selected or contracted with an OTA-accredited Fawtara Application Service Provider (ASP) to handle transmission, validation, and OTA response management?
Section 3 of 4

Process Maturity

Q5Have you mapped and tested e-invoicing workflows for complex transaction types — credit notes, self-billing, intercompany invoices, FX-denominated transactions, and future B2G flows?
Q6Do you have a documented plan for ongoing Fawtara compliance operations post go-live — OTA response handling, rejection resolution, reconciliation, data-integrity checks, and staff capacity?
Section 4 of 4

Organisational & Strategic Readiness

Q7Has your organisation secured a formally approved, fully scoped budget for Fawtara — covering ERP changes, ASP fees, internal IT time, testing, training, and post-go-live support?
Q8How is your organisation approaching the strategic value of Fawtara beyond compliance — real-time invoice data for working-capital optimisation, continuous audit readiness, or finance automation?
