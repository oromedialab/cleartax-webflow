Key requirements

Germany's e-invoicing at a glance

What is changing, who is in scope, and how e-invoices move under the German mandate.

Model

Decentralised model

No central government portal. Invoices are exchanged directly between businesses, via Peppol, EDI or email by agreement, using the EN 16931 European standard.

Scope

Who is in scope

Domestic B2B between businesses established in Germany. Receiving is mandatory now; issuing applies from 2027 (>€800k turnover) and all B2B from 2028.

Format

Accepted formats

XRechnung (structured XML) and ZUGFeRD (hybrid PDF/A-3 + XML), both EN 16931-compliant. A plain PDF or paper invoice does not count.

Reporting

Exchange only, for now

The German mandate covers issuing and exchange. There is no real-time transaction reporting yet, EU-wide digital reporting arrives later with ViDA.

Archival

8-year retention

Structured invoices must be retained in original form for 8 years, kept legible, unaltered and available for tax audit.

Authority

Listed on VeR

ClearTax is listed on VeR (Verband elektronische Rechnung), Germany's leading e-invoicing association, aligned with German and EU requirements as they evolve.
