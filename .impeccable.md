## Design Context

### Users
B2B finance decision-makers (CFOs, Tax Heads, Finance Directors) working at mid-to-large UAE enterprises preparing for the 2026–27 e-invoicing mandate. They arrive via LinkedIn, email invites, or Cleartax CRM touchpoints — usually on desktop during work hours, sometimes mobile in evenings. Jobs-to-be-done differ by page type: event/roundtable pages = evaluate whether to attend and RSVP; product/mandate pages = understand deadlines, assess vendors, justify internal buy-in.

### Brand Personality
Trustworthy, compliance-grade, urgent-but-composed. Three words: **precise · authoritative · current**. Cleartax sells to regulated enterprises — the voice has to earn senior-finance trust on the first scroll. No playful toy-like gestures. No salesy hype. Earn the RSVP like a KPMG invite, not a webinar landing page.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Baseline**: editorial-corporate. Closer to *Stripe Sessions / Stripe.com* than SaaS landing pages. Crisp typography, asymmetric layouts, generous whitespace, a single restrained accent used with discipline (60-30-10 rule).
- **Brand palette**: primary `--color-dark: #171E28`, surfaces `#1E2738`, brand accent `--color-purple: #6355D8`. Event/roundtable pages may swap in gold `#C9A961` as the prestige accent.
- **Theme per context**: product/mandate pages lean light for trust; invite-only event pages lean dark for prestige and evening-gala feel. Mixed light/dark sections are welcome when a page covers both (deadline urgency + exclusive attendance).
- **Typography**: brand fonts **Nohemi Bold** for display/headings and **Gilroy Medium** for body (licensed via Webflow; fallback `Manrope, sans-serif` in local previews). No Inter, Plus Jakarta, Playfair, or any other reflex font.
- **Anti-references**: generic webinar LPs (Hubspot, Demio), SaaS pricing pages (Pipedrive, Monday), AI-slop decks (gradient-text headlines, cyan-on-dark neon, glassmorphic stacks of cards with rounded icon tiles).
- **Graphics**: inline SVG for decoration (grid, hairlines, diagrammatic marks, numbered badges). Unsplash photography for hero contexts — Dubai skyline / boardroom / finance interiors — used sparingly (max one hero image per page). Never stock-photo collages.

### Design Principles
1. **Trust over flourish.** Every decorative element has to survive the question "would KPMG ship this?" Cut it if not.
2. **Rhythm through restraint.** Tight groupings (8–12px) within cards, generous separation between sections (80–128px). Never uniform padding.
3. **One accent per screen.** Gold or purple, not both. Accents work because they are rare.
4. **Asymmetry beats center-alignment.** Left-aligned copy with asymmetric grids feels authored; centered columns feel templated.
5. **Content-first typography.** Nohemi at 1.25 ratio steps (or tighter for dense UI). Body capped at 65–75ch. No gradient text, no uppercase paragraphs.

### Project-specific rules
- Brand colors lock to `src/styles/tokens.css`. Event pages may override with gold `#C9A961` as accent, never replace brand purple globally.
- No RTL even though audience is UAE-based — user explicitly confirmed.
- Files in `archive/` are standalone single-file HTML experiments, outside the Astro build. Keep them paste-ready with inlined `<style>` and `<script>`.
- WCAG AA minimum.
